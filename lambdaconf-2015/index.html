Call for Speakers

At LamdbaConf 2014, one-hundred and thirty functional programmers assembled from around the world in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains to take their skills to the next level.

Skills and friendships were forged in a day of intense workshops and talks, covering a variety of languages including Scala, Haskell, Clojure, Racket, Idris, and Purescript.

At LambdaConf 2015, we're shooting for more attendees, more lightning talks, more workshops, more talks, and more cross-pollination between developers from many different backgrounds.

Please contact us if you have any one of the following topics you'd like to share with fellow enthusiasts:

  • Languages. Want to cover a functional or logic programming language? From Scala to Swift to Prolog to ATS and Rust, we want to open people's mind to new languages, or new ways to use old languages in a functional or declarative way.
  • FP 101. Interested in helping object-oriented, procedural, and FP-curious programmers learn a new trick or two in the world of functional programming? Beginner-friendly workshops and talks help grow the size of the community.
  • Libraries. The ecosystem of functional libraries is exploding for many languages, like Haskell, Purescript, and Scala. Is there a library you know well that can help developers solve real-world problems, and demonstrate the power of functional programming?
  • Theory. Homotopy type theory, category theory, F-algebras — all of these and more have helped developers write safer, more generic, more comprehensible code. Can you make one of these topics accessible and relevant to functional programmers? Advanced topics and ongoing research welcome, too!
  • Case Studies. Has a functional programming language or library done great things for your company? Present a case study that discusses how you solved some problem in a functional or declarative way.

Lightning talks are between 5 - 10 minutes in length, and take place Friday night during the conference dinner. The atmosphere is very informal and we welcome those without prior speaking experience.

Friday workshops are 1:50 hours in length (with a 10 minute break at the halfway point), and are expected to have a hands-on component.

Saturday talks are between 25 minutes (for a half hour session) and 50 minutes (for an hour session), and may include interactive components at the speaker's discretion.

Speakers for all sessions other than lightning talks get free conference registration, travel assistance (dependent on sponsors), and an awesome LambdaConf hoodie designed just for speakers.

The Call for Speakers closes February 27, but speakers are encouraged to submit proposals as early as possible to reserve space.

Airport

Boulder is served by the Denver International Airport (symbol: DEN).

Low-cost national carriers into Denver include Frontier Airlines and Southwest. These carriers fly from most major cities in the United States, and you can often find a round-trip fare for around $200.

British Airways has direct, non-stop flights from London Heathrow to Denver.

The best way of getting from the airport to Boulder is the RTD Boulder AB Bus Route. The bus takes about an hour and drops you off at the Boulder Transit Station, which is located in the heart of downtown Boulder, and a short walk away from our recommended hotels.

Unless you will be staying outside of Boulder, you probably do not need to rent a car. Boulder is extremely walkable and bikable and also has excellent public transit options.

Volunteer

Are you a student? Or maybe just flat broke but absolutely have to attend LambdaConf, anyway?

We're looking for a few good volunteers! Volunteers get free conference passes in exchange for help organizing the conference. Typical responsibilities include:

  • Promotion. Notifying newsgroups, websites, and meetups about the conference.
  • Facilitation. Help out at the signup tables, help speakers with A/V equipment, and help direct attendees around the facilities.
  • Setup & Cleanup. Help setup, tear down, and cleanup for meals and the conference dinner.
  • Sponsorships. Reach out to selected companies to obtain sponsorships to help defray the costs of organizing the conference.

In addition to a free conference pass, LambdaConf volunteers get an awesome hoodie designed just for volunteers.

Apply to become a volunteer by sending us a signed copy of the Volunteer Pledge, as well as a brief description of how you want to help make LambdaConf 2015 an event to remember!

The Volunteer Pledge

I hereby acknowledge that I am being granted status as a LambdaConf volunteer, in exchange for my assistance helping to organize the conference, including such duties as promotion, setup and cleanup, search for sponsorships, and facilitation, as agreed between myself and the conference organizers.

In my capacity as volunteer of LambdaConf, I hereby promise:

  • That I shall not talk or act in ways that could make people different than me feel bullied, harassed, intimidated, stalked, stereotyped, or belittled;
  • That I will embrace and celebrate the diversity of all minority groups, including women, people of color, disabled individuals, lesbians, gays, bisexuals, transsexuals, asexuals, intersex people, transgender people, and gender-variant people; and that I will show no biases towards anyone on the basis of these differences;
  • That I will treat everyone with the same measure of respect and dignity, and that in every word I speak and every action I take, I will show genuine concern for helping others achieve their goals;
  • That if I become aware of any behavior by others which is inconsistent with these promises, I will take immediate action to report such behavior to the conference organizers;
  • That if there comes a time when I no longer wish to keep these promises, or I behave in a manner inconsistent with these promises, I shall immediately step down as a LambdaConf volunteer.

I hereby acknowledge that my status as LambdaConf volunteer may be terminated at any time if, in the sole opinion of the conference organizers, I have been determined to break any of these promises.

Code of Conduct

LambdaConf's code of conduct is simple: be a decent human being.

Celebrate and embrace diversity, treat everyone with respect and dignity, and in the words you speak and the actions you take, show genuine care and concern for helping others achieve their goals.

We have a zero tolerance policy towards written or spoken words, behaviors, artifacts, and media that make minority groups feel bullied, harassed, intimidated, stalked, stereotyped, or belittled. These groups include the following: women, people of color, disabled individuals, lesbians, gays, bisexuals, transsexuals, asexuals, intersex people, transgender people, and gender-variant people.

Violations of this code of conduct should be reported immediately to conference staff or volunteers. All staff and volunteers will be wearing Team LambdaConf apparel.
